Hannah has been shooting expansive scenes of Mother Nature her whole life. Born in Melbourne, Australia to hardworking Czech immigrants, she never took the gift of life's beauty for granted. Hannah's catalogue of work can be traced back to the essential moment when, at 8 years of age, the gift of a Brownie Kodak camera from her parents set her on a course for destiny. Since her first, fateful snapshot of a spider web in the family garden, Hannah has followed her calling to capture and share the most beautiful places on earth.

It was after a trip to the United States in 1984, that Hannah discovered the panoramic camera format, a defining moment in her career. Challenged with the dare to "go big or go home," Hannah did both. For the next two decades Hannah would travel back and forth between the United States and her native Australia, honing her craft and establishing her prominent position in the world of fine art photography.
